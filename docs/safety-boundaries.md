# Safety Boundaries

CareBridge Canada is a healthcare communication, organization, education, and follow-up support tool.

It is not a medical decision-making system.

## CareBridge can help with

CareBridge can help users:

- prepare for appointments
- summarize their own notes
- understand instructions in plain language
- generate questions for clinicians or pharmacists
- track referrals, labs, imaging, medications, and follow-ups
- create family/caregiver summaries
- translate simplified care instructions
- prepare doctor-ready and pharmacist-ready handoffs
- remember unresolved questions

## CareBridge must not do

CareBridge must not:

- diagnose conditions
- recommend treatment
- approve prescriptions
- tell users to start, stop, or change medications
- provide emergency triage
- replace a doctor, nurse, pharmacist, hospital, clinic, or provincial health system
- secretly record clinicians
- store real patient data in early prototypes
- ask for health card numbers in the MVP
- connect to government health portals in the MVP

## Safe wording

Use wording like:

- Here are topics to discuss with your clinician.
- Here are questions you may want to ask.
- Here are follow-up items to track.
- Here is a plain-language summary of your instructions.
- This may be worth following up with your clinic, pharmacist, or healthcare provider.

Avoid wording like:

- You have this disease.
- Take this treatment.
- This prescription is safe.
- Ignore this symptom.
- This is definitely urgent.
- You do not need to see a doctor.

## Emergency safety

CareBridge should not attempt to determine whether a situation is an emergency.

For emergency-like user input, the app should direct users to seek local emergency care or contact appropriate health services.

## Clinician consent

CareBridge should never be designed around secret recording.

Any visit recording workflow must require explicit clinician consent.

MVP transcript workflows should use pasted or simulated transcripts only.
